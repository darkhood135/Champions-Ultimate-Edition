Deadpool

Wade Wilson

Berserker

A dying mercenary who traded his death for a healing factor that now refuses to let him have it, Wade Wilson talks, kills, and absolutely will not stay down as the Merc with a Mouth — Deadpool.

Wade Wilson was a dying man who agreed to become a lab rat, and he got exactly what he asked for and nothing he wanted. The program that cured his terminal cancer did it by giving him a healing factor that fights the disease to an endless stalemate — leaving his body a ruin of scar tissue that dies and rebuilds itself forever, and his mind scrambled past any hope of repair. What's left is the loudest man in any room and, beneath the noise, easily the saddest. The jokes never stop because they can't; they're the armor between Wade and a bottomless well of self-loathing over what he looks like, what he's done for money, and how few people can stand to be near him for long. He wants to be a hero. He idolizes the real ones with an earnestness that would be touching if it weren't so uncomfortable, and he keeps hurling himself at the mark and falling just short of it — a killer trying, clumsily and sincerely, to become something better. He talks to people who aren't there, knows things no one has told him, and addresses an audience nobody else can see. And for all the laughing, the cruelest joke belongs to him: Wade Wilson longs for death more than anything, and death is the one thing his body will never allow.

That same curse makes him a nightmare to fight. Wade regenerates from wounds that would erase anyone else — bullets, blades, dismemberment, all of it inconvenient rather than fatal — and shrugs off poison, disease, and any attempt to enter a mind that reshapes itself faster than a telepath can read it. He is a genuinely elite combatant beneath the clowning, a master of firearms and blades who lives with a pair of katanas on his back and a working knowledge of every weapon ever made. Worst of all for anyone across from him, he cannot be predicted, planned for, or reasoned with — he simply keeps coming, cheerfully, long after any sane opponent would have stopped. He is unkillable, and it isn't a gift. It's a sentence — and Wade serves it out loud, cracking jokes the whole way, still hoping that one of these days he'll get to be one of the good guys.

"Gentlemen. So nice of you to visit. Which of you scheduled the vivisection today?"

Regeneration Buddies w/ Wolverine

As far as Wade is concerned, the healing factor Weapon X built out of Logan's makes the two of them family — brothers, practically, whether the old man likes it or not. He does not. Wade has never let that slow him down: since neither of them has the decency to die, he treats their brawls as quality time and Logan's threats as terms of endearment, and he'll keep turning up no matter how many times he gets gutted for it. Beneath all the mouth is the thing Wade would never say straight — that Logan is exactly the kind of hero he has never quite managed to become, and one word of approval from him would mean more than Wade could stand to admit.

Shoot Shoot Pow Pow w/ The Punisher

Wade is convinced he and Frank Castle are two of a kind, and says so constantly, at length, over the sound of gunfire. Frank has never once agreed. To Wade it's a dream pairing — all the firepower, none of the lectures — so he chatters into a silence that never answers, entirely unbothered by a man who'd shoot him purely to make it stop. But there's a reason he keeps circling back: Wade recognizes someone who lost everything and built a war out of the wreckage, because he did the very same thing and just decided to laugh instead. Frank never laughs.

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.1.0 July 11, 2026
  • Rework Trickshot: accuracy penalty reduced −120% → −100% ACC, and accuracy gained per cooldown turn ignored increased +15% → +20%. Clarified that this accuracy scaling still functions while "Overdrive" is active (using the ability's attached cooldown number, at no HP cost). Turns Trickshot from a near-unusable cliff into an achievable high-risk burst — roughly two turns of Waiting to bank enough cooldown for a guaranteed shot.
  • Buff Hey, Champs! (Core): "Waiting" now increases Trickshot's cooldown by 3 turns (up from 2). Terminology also corrected from "Resting" to the proper basic action, "Waiting."
  • Rework Don't Stop Me Now and All for One: their "cannot receive healing" windows now explicitly block the reversed damage-to-HP from Breaking Conventions. These self-sacrifice drawbacks can no longer be bypassed by an ally damaging Deadpool during the anti-heal window.
These fixes make Deadpool's cooldown-ignoring engine functional and fair without touching his ceiling. Trickshot now has a real accuracy ramp instead of an impossible one, and his self-sacrifice drawbacks (the 1%-HP ULTIMATUM, the Don't Stop Me Now buff) can no longer be trivially undone by allied damage via Breaking Conventions. The "Overdrive" status was updated globally to specify that cooldowns still reset upon use — preventing multiple empowered Trickshots in a single Overdrive turn — and the cooldown-ignoring mechanic is being added to the Catechism as a defined term.
