Batman

Bruce Wayne

Breaker

Forged by the murder of his parents into a lifelong war on crime, billionaire Bruce Wayne stalks the night as Batman — the Dark Knight of Gotham City and the world's greatest detective.

Batman was born the night a boy knelt in a Gotham alley beside his dying mother and father and swore, in his grief, that no one else would ever suffer as he had. Everything Bruce Wayne became after was built on that oath. He is not a man who was given power but one who forged himself into it — driving a brilliant, traumatized mind and an ordinary human body through decades of relentless discipline until fear itself became his to command. He wages his war by a single unbreakable rule, refusing to take the life that would make him indistinguishable from the killers he hunts, and that line is the last thread tethering the mission to the man. To the underworld he is a myth made to haunt them; to those who know him, "Bruce Wayne" is the mask and the Bat is the truth beneath it — a figure of pure, obsessive will who does not rest, does not break, and will not stop until his city is safe, even knowing that day may never come.

He does all of it without a single power. What Batman possesses instead is a body honed to the absolute peak of human capability and a mind sharper still — a master of a dozen martial arts, the finest detective alive, and a strategist so thorough he keeps a contingency for every threat imaginable, including his closest allies. Cowl and cape turn fear into a weapon; his vast fortune arms him with technology no one else can field, and the shadows do the rest. But the gadgets and the fortune were never what made him formidable. It's the will behind them — the refusal of one ordinary man to ever be powerless again, sharpened into something that makes gods and monsters check the darkness before they move.

"Ladies. Gentlemen. You have eaten well. You've eaten Gotham's wealth. Its spirit. Your feast is nearly over. From this moment on...none of you are safe."

A Familiar Silhouette w/ Black Cat

Batman has seen this act before — the charm, the acrobatics, the cat burglar who treats a guarded vault as an invitation and a smile as an alibi — and he has precisely no patience left for any of it. What unsettles him isn't that Felicia Hardy is a puzzle; it's that she isn't. He knows every move before she makes it, and he knows just as well where the blind spot in his own judgment sits when it comes to women who move the way she does. He would never admit that the reason he hasn't simply put her away is that he has walked this road before, and it did not end the way he expected.

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.2 July 11, 2026
  • Nerf Durability pass — MAX HP reduced 10,800 → 7,500, DEF 190 → 120, EVA 35% → 15%.

As a Breaker — a disruptive, intentionally fragile role — Batman was carrying tank-tier durability: the highest DEF in the roster stacked with a large HP pool and high evasion, with only low RES as a weakness. Against physical damage he was effectively unkillable while still dealing high burst, which broke the disruptor's risk/reward. This pass pulls him into line with the fragile-disruptor archetype (comparable to the roster's other Breaker), keeping his melee-dodge lean (15% base + Reinforced Body Armor's +15% vs melee) and magic vulnerability (RES 80) while removing the "durable and high-damage with no tradeoff" profile. His disruption and burst are untouched — now genuinely paid for by fragility.

v1.0.1 May 21, 2026
  • Buff Vengeance (ULTIMATUM 1) — base damage raised from 400% → 500% ATK, with the conditional bonus also raised from +400% → +500% ATK. Maximum potential damage now caps at 1,000% ATK against a low-HP target.
  • Nerf Predator (Special — Reserve) — range reduced from 20 → 14 panels.
  • Rework The Batmobile (ULTIMATUM — Reserve) — clarified targeting: Batman charges 8 panels in any chosen direction from self, sweeping through a 3-panel-wide line and hitting all enemies caught in it.
  • Rework CEO of Wayne Enterprises (Leader) — clarified wording from "and" to "or" between the two qualifying links (an ally only needs Bat-Family or Tactical Mind to receive the buff, not both).

Vengeance was meager outside its execute condition and worth a base raise; the conditional now also scales harder to keep it competitive with other single-target ULTIMATUMs. Predator's old 20-range was the longest in the game and a bit dominating; 14 keeps the long-range stalker fantasy while tuning down extreme-distance application of Terrify. The Batmobile's "3x8" range was ambiguous; the new wording defines the cone and direction concretely. CEO's "and" → "or" prevents a common-sense misread where readers might assume an ally needs both link tags.
