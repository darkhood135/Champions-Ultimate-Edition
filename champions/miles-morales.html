Spider-Man (Miles Morales)

Miles Morales

Breaker

A Brooklyn teenager bitten by a stolen, genetically altered spider, Miles Morales took up the name of a fallen hero and made it his own as the Spider-Man of a new generation.

Miles never wanted the mask — he inherited it, pulling it on in the shadow of a legend and spending every day since trying to prove he was worthy of a name that once belonged to someone else. What makes him singular isn't that he followed Peter Parker, but that he refused to simply be him: a good kid from a loving home, torn between the father who wears a badge and mistrusts men in masks, the mother who anchors him, and the uncle whose criminal life showed him exactly the person he's determined never to become. He carries the ordinary weight of a teenager — school, family, the ache of not measuring up — alongside the impossible one, and answers both with a warmth and earnestness the older heroes have long since had beaten out of them. Where his predecessor is defined by guilt, Miles is defined by heart: the constant, hopeful effort of a young man learning that living up to a legacy means becoming something new, not something borrowed.

That effort is backed by a power set that echoes the original and then breaks from it. Miles has the proportionate strength, agility, wall-crawling, and danger-sense of the spider, but the stolen serum that changed him left two gifts wholly his own — a "venom blast" of bio-electric energy that can stun a foe or drop him cold with a touch, and a camouflage that bends light and sound around him until he vanishes from sight entirely. Together they make him something no Spider-Man was before: a hero who can strike from nowhere and end a fight in a heartbeat. But the powers were never what set him apart. It's that he took a borrowed name and a borrowed dream and, swing by swing, proved they were always meant to be his.

"I'm not an Avenger. I'm not Thor. Or Cap. Or Peter. I'm just a kid in a mask. Doing the best I can. But I'll never give up on the world. Or this city. Or anyone that needs me. I'm Miles Morales. And I'm Spider-Man."

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.2 July 11, 2026
  • Nerf Durability pass — MAX HP reduced 8,900 → 7,300, DEF 160 → 100, RES 140 → 90, EVA 30% → 20%.
  • Rework Electrified Web — extension clause reworded for clarity: "extends into itself once for every 2 full turns since its last use (maximum of two extensions)." No functional change. The conditional Paralyze line was also tidied.
  • Rework Spiders In Sync (Wearing the Mask synergy) — now functions as two independent self-passives (each Spider grants their own copy) rather than mutually granting, preventing the buff from double-applying when both Spiders are on the team.

As one of the earliest Champions, Miles carried tank-tier durability that didn't fit his Breaker role — after Spider-Man's pass, Miles was the tankier of the two Spiders despite being the fragile-disruptor archetype. Cutting HP, both defenses and EVA turns him into a proper Breaker: his Shock/Paralyze lockdown, evasion, and stealth identity are fully intact, but he now pays for that disruption with survivability and can be answered by focusing him down. His fragility is also the natural check on his fast Shock-fueled ULT charging, so no cap was added. Wording cleanups preserve original intent.

v1.0.1 May 20, 2026
  • Buff Doing Things a Different Way (Leader) — Web Warrior allies' infliction chance bonus increased from +5% → +8%.
  • Rework Quick Swing — replaced the unclear "+15% SPD for this ability" with +30 SPD before this turn's initiative is calculated, applying the boost in time to reorder Miles earlier this turn. Reinforces the ability's identity as a panic-button escape tool.
  • Rework Learning Responsibility (Passive 2) — added a stacking ceiling of +20% maximum on the ally-success ATK/MAG buff so it can't snowball uncapped in long fights.

Leader buff was undertuned relative to Peter's. Quick Swing's priority concept needed wording that didn't rely on a private per-ability stat — a one-turn SPD boost applied pre-initiative respects the normal turn-order system while letting Miles bail out of bad positions. Learning Responsibility's intent was always to stack, but with a 6-character team triggering it every turn the uncapped version could climb to runaway numbers; 20% caps it at a meaningful but bounded buff.

v1.0.0 May 5, 2026
