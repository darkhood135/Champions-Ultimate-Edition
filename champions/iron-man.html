Iron Man

Anthony Stark

Blaster

A genius industrialist who built a suit of armor to escape captivity and never truly took it off, Tony Stark now defends the world he once armed as the invincible Iron Man.

Tony Stark spent the first half of his life building the weapons that made him rich, and the whole of the second half trying to answer for them. It took a battlefield ambush, a chest full of shrapnel, and a cave to change him — held captive and ordered to build for his captors, he instead built his escape alongside a man named Yinsen, who died so that he could walk out of it. Stark came home understanding at last what his creations had been doing to people who never asked for a war, and he has been paying that debt down ever since. What defines him is the futurist's curse: a mind that sees the catastrophe coming years before anyone else does and cannot rest until it's solved — a compulsion that has saved the world more than once and, in his arrogance and need for control, nearly torn it apart just as often. He is no clean-cut hero and has never claimed to be, having wrestled publicly with his own ego, his failures, and the bottle that once brought the great Tony Stark to his knees. But he is a man who rebuilds himself as relentlessly as he rebuilds his armor, and however far he falls, he always comes back with something better than what he had before.

That relentlessness is worn on the outside. The Iron Man armor is the finest technology on Earth, an ever-evolving shell that grants him flight at incredible speed, strength and durability far beyond any man's, and an arsenal built around the repulsors in his palms and the unibeam at his chest — backed by sensors, AI, and a hangar of specialized suits designed for every threat he can imagine, from the ocean floor to deep space. Behind it all is the true weapon: one of the most brilliant minds alive, coupled with the resources of an empire. Yet the armor was never what made him Iron Man. It's the man sealed inside it — flawed, driven, and stubbornly unwilling to be remembered as the merchant of death he started out as.

"There's only two things I ever managed to believe in. Firstly, myself. And even then only about 50% of the time. Secondly, the future. That there would be one and we'd make it."

Conviction and Compromise w/ Captain America

Tony has never stopped measuring himself against Steve Rogers, and has never quite believed he measures up. Together they built the Avengers into something the world could rely on — the futurist's vision married to the soldier's conviction — but when Tony saw catastrophe coming and chose control over conscience, it cost him his oldest friend and very nearly his soul. He would rather be hated and right than loved and wrong, and he has never forgiven himself for how thoroughly he once proved it.

A Trust Rebuilt w/ Spider-Man

In Peter Parker, Tony found the rarest thing of all — a mind that could keep pace with his own, wrapped around a far better heart. He wanted to hand the kid every advantage he'd never had: the armor, the resources, a seat at the table. Instead he spent that trust pushing Peter into the light, a decision that cost the young man nearly everything, and he has been quietly trying to make it right ever since — mentor to the one protégé whose good opinion he genuinely cares about keeping.

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.1.0 May 30, 2026
  • Rework Legion of Armors — now activates after a 2-turn delay (was immediate); active duration reduced from 2 turns to 1 turn; added clarification that suits do not replicate extensions layered within abilities.
  • Rework Homing Missiles — base damage increased from 600% to 650% MAG; obstacle ACC penalty increased from −40% to −60%; EN restoration capped at 40 (was uncapped).
  • Nerf Genius with Few Limitations — ULT generation from enemy damage no longer applies to extension attacks.
  • Rework Unibeam — range corrected from 8 to 5 panels; typo in ability text fixed.
  • Rework Energy Slash — clarified that the EN and STA restore condition checks the enemy's proximity at the start of Iron Man's turn specifically.
  • Rework Melee Protocol — clarified that the additional ATK bonus triggers at each exact 10% HP lost threshold.

Legion of Armors' immediate 2-turn burst window created an unavoidable damage spike with no counterplay — the 2-turn activation delay and 1-turn window allow opponents to respond through repositioning, particularly behind obstacles, which Homing Missiles' increased ACC penalty further rewards. The ULT generation nerf corrects an uncapped compounding issue where Repulsor Blast's extension chain could charge Legion of Armors twice as fast as intended. The Homing Missiles damage increase partially offsets the EN restore cap, keeping the ability's single-use value competitive while removing the EN-positive loop on large groups.
