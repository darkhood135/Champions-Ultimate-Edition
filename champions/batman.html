Bruce Wayne

Breaker

Bruce Wayne is the costume. Batman is the man — and the man is preparation. A detective by obsession, a martial artist by drill, a billionaire by inheritance, he traded a normal life for a permanent war against the evil that took his parents. He has no powers. Every gadget on his belt, every contingency for every enemy, has been studied and refined. He fights the way he investigates: patient, methodical, three moves ahead. He waits in the dark until criminals are too afraid to look up, then ends it before they realize he was there. The cowardly and the superstitious have a name for the shape they fear — and it isn't Bruce Wayne.

"Ladies. Gentlemen. You have eaten well. You've eaten Gotham's wealth. Its spirit. Your feast is nearly over. From this moment on...none of you are safe."

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.1 May 21, 2026
  • Buff Vengeance (ULTIMATUM 1) — base damage raised from 400% → 500% ATK, with the conditional bonus also raised from +400% → +500% ATK. Maximum potential damage now caps at 1,000% ATK against a low-HP target.
  • Nerf Predator (Special — Reserve) — range reduced from 20 → 14 panels.
  • Rework The Batmobile (ULTIMATUM — Reserve) — clarified targeting: Batman charges 8 panels in any chosen direction from self, sweeping through a 3-panel-wide line and hitting all enemies caught in it.
  • Rework CEO of Wayne Enterprises (Leader) — clarified wording from "and" to "or" between the two qualifying links (an ally only needs Bat-Family or Tactical Mind to receive the buff, not both).

Vengeance was meager outside its execute condition and worth a base raise; the conditional now also scales harder to keep it competitive with other single-target ULTIMATUMs. Predator's old 20-range was the longest in the game and a bit dominating; 14 keeps the long-range stalker fantasy while tuning down extreme-distance application of Terrify. The Batmobile's "3x8" range was ambiguous; the new wording defines the cone and direction concretely. CEO's "and" → "or" prevents a common-sense misread where readers might assume an ally needs both link tags.
