Black Cat

Felicia Hardy

Plunderer

The daughter of a legendary cat burglar who set out to surpass him, Felicia Hardy prowls the rooftops as the Black Cat — a thief of extraordinary skill who answers to no rules but her own.

Felicia Hardy didn't fall into the life; she chose it, and then she trained until she was better at it than the man who inspired her. Raised idolizing a father whose reputation as a master thief outlived his freedom, she set out to eclipse him — and after a violation in her youth left her swearing she would never again be at anyone's mercy, she built herself into someone who never would be. What drives her has never really been the money. It's the rush: the sealed vault, the impossible rooftop, the thrill of taking something everyone swore couldn't be taken. That appetite has landed her on every side of the line at one point or another — thief, rival, reluctant ally, and once or twice something considerably worse — and she is entirely unbothered by the inconsistency, because she keeps to a code of her own making rather than anyone else's. Beneath the flirtation and the swagger is real warmth and a fierce loyalty to the very few people she lets close, chief among them a certain wall-crawler she fell for in the mask long before she had any interest in the man beneath it — a tangle neither of them has ever entirely worked out.

She does it all on skill she earned rather than luck she was handed. Felicia is an Olympic-caliber acrobat and a formidable martial artist, a peerless infiltrator who can defeat any lock, alarm, or security system ever devised, moving through a guarded building like she was never there at all. Retractable claws let her scale sheer walls and tear through steel, a grapple line carries her across the skyline, and enhanced reflexes and night vision make the dark her home ground. Most dangerous of all is the misfortune that trails in her wake — a field of pure bad luck she can turn on an enemy, so that weapons jam, footing gives way, and the odds quietly stop being theirs. Whether the Black Cat is a hero, a criminal, or something in between depends entirely on the night and on what's in the safe — and she wouldn't have it any other way.

"I'm not a hero. I'm a thief. Born a thief. Raised a thief. Will die a thief."

Never Quite Caught w/ Spider-Man

Felicia fell for the mask long before she had any use for the man beneath it — the thrill of a partner who could match her stride for stride across a rooftop, who traded quips while the alarms screamed, who made the whole dangerous game feel like it had been built for the two of them. Learning his name spoiled that, and neither has ever fully recovered the balance since; they've been lovers, partners, rivals, and for one bitter stretch something considerably worse. Still she keeps circling back. He's the one person who ever tempted her to be better than she is, and the one she has never stopped trying to tempt in the other direction.

A Familiar Silhouette w/ Batman

Felicia took one look at the Dark Knight and saw the most interesting challenge of her career — a man with no weakness she can find, nothing she can charm, and a city positively full of things she'd like to steal. Batman, to her considerable irritation, was not remotely impressed. He has met her kind before, knows every step of this particular dance by heart, and long ago ran out of patience for cat burglars who think a smile constitutes an alibi. She'll keep at it regardless. It's the first time in years the game has been genuinely difficult, and she has no intention of walking away from that.

Nothing Worth the Bullet w/ The Punisher

Felicia is one of the very few in the criminal world who understands precisely what Frank Castle is, which is exactly why she gives him the widest berth she can manage. He knows who she is, too, and has never once come for her — because for all she has taken over the years, she has never put a body in the ground, and Frank does not waste bullets on thieves. It's an understanding rather than an alliance: she keeps her hands clean and stays well out of his way, he tolerates her existence, and both of them know exactly what happens the day she crosses that line.

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.1 July 11, 2026
  • Nerf The Master Thief (Core Passive) — ULT gain per stolen buff reduced 6% → 5% and limited to once per ability (previously triggered on every individual buff stolen).
  • Nerf The Master Thief (Core Passive) — Stealth evasion bonus reduced +35% → +25% EVA.
  • Nerf MAX HP reduced 8,600 → 8,000. DEF reduced 113 → 110.
  • Rework Whip Slash and Whip of Web — targeting line rewritten to match roster convention: "Target: All Champions in 3-Wide Line · Range: 2 panels (chosen direction)" (previously the non-standard "Range: 2x3"). No functional change.

Black Cat's theft engine was charging her ULTIMATUM far faster than intended. At 6% per individual buff stolen, a single turn of Sleight of Hand under "Stealth" (three buffs) plus Breaking Trust (two buffs) generated roughly 30% ULT, putting Feline Fury's 850% ATK payoff on a three-to-four turn cycle — several times the rate of comparable trigger-based ULT generation elsewhere in the roster. Moving to 5% once per ability keeps stealing as her charge source without letting multi-buff abilities multiply it. The durability trim addresses a Plunderer — explicitly the risky, abrasive, selfish role — sitting above every other agile street-level Champion in HP while also carrying a very large conditional dodge; at 8,000 HP with a 25% Stealth bonus she is still slippery, but her greed now carries real exposure. DEF was also rounded to match roster convention, and the Whip Slash targeting wording was standardised.
