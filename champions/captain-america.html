Captain America

Steve Rogers

Defender

Steve Rogers was chosen for the Super-Soldier program not because he was strong, but because he already was what a soldier should be — honest, stubborn, and impossible to keep down. The serum brought his body in line with his will: peak-human strength, reflexes that put trained fighters behind the reaction curve, and a recovery rate that turns punishment into arithmetic. The vibranium shield is his most versatile tool — thrown to chain through enemy formations, raised to negate incoming fire, or planted as a line he dares anyone to cross. His tactics center on his team. He reads the battlefield, sets the tempo, and makes sure every ally is resourced and in position when the real work starts. He does not outpower most of what he faces. He out-endures it — and he makes his side considerably harder to kill.

"I'll never stop believing that life is something most sacred, something to be sacrificed only for the greatest of causes...liberty!"

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.1.0 May 26, 2026
  • Nerf Shield Throw — Cooldown increased from 1 → 2 turns.
  • Rework Prepare to Engage — Removed the "30% chance to inflict Retribution on all attacks for 2 turns" line. DEF buff expanded to include RES: now grants +20% DEF and RES for 2 turns.
  • Nerf Stars and Stripes — Per-hit damage reduced from 220% → 200% ATK (600% ATK total, down from 660%).
  • Rework No Soldier Left Behind — Removed the infliction chance scaling line. Added a formation bonus: +10% DEF and RES while no ally is below 30% HP or defeated. Added +5% ATK per ally below 30% HP (includes defeated allies) alongside the existing +7% DEF and RES scaling.
  • Rework No Surrender — Fully redesigned. Now a self-targeted support ULTIMATUM: cleanses all negative effects, grants Indestructible for 2 turns and Taunt for 3 turns, and counters every attack received for 200% ATK damage to the attacker for 3 turns. Previous version dealt 600–1200% ATK damage to a single target.
  • Rework Vibranium Shield — Wording clarified. Now reads: "Reduces damage of incoming ranged attacks by 40% while not blocking, waiting, or affected by a movement- or action-restricting infliction." No mechanical change.

Shield Throw's 1-turn cooldown allowed near-constant AoE pressure for its energy cost on a Defender, pushing it above the roster's multi-target efficiency baseline. Prepare to Engage had excessive Retribution density when combined with Shield Bash and Stars and Stripes; trading that proc for a full DEF/RES buff preserves the ability's value as a defensive stance without the control overlap. Stars and Stripes was an outlier in damage-per-energy for the role given that each hit calculates DEF separately. No Soldier Left Behind gained a formation bonus to create an incentive for proactive ally protection alongside the existing comeback scaling. No Surrender was redesigned to offer a distinct ULTIMATUM identity — a personal last-stand button — that competes meaningfully with Assemble in solo-carry and heavy-focus scenarios rather than duplicating its team-support function.
