Robin

Damian Wayne

Booster

The son of Batman and heir to the League of Assassins, bred from infancy to be the perfect weapon, Damian Wayne fights to earn a nobler name as Robin — the Boy Wonder.

Damian Wayne was engineered to be a killer before he was ever allowed to be a child. Son of Bruce Wayne and Talia al Ghul, grandson of the immortal Ra's al Ghul, he was raised in the League of Assassins as its groomed heir — schooled in a hundred ways to end a life and taught to believe the world was his by blood. When he finally came to his father, he arrived as an arrogant, lethal boy who thought the mantle of Robin was owed to him, and who took life without hesitation or regret. What makes him remarkable is everything that came after: the slow, furious struggle to become something his makers never intended. Beneath the sneer and the entitlement is a child at war with his own nature, straining against a heritage soaked in blood and reaching — clumsily, proudly, and with a buried desperation for his father's approval — toward the harder path of the hero. He is learning, one spared life at a time, the lesson his father built a war around: that the name of Robin cannot be inherited or seized, only earned.

For all that he is still a boy, Damian fights with the honed lethality of a master many times his age. The League forged him into a peerless martial artist and a deadly hand with the blade, fluent in every weapon and killing art, and to that inheritance he adds the Wayne genius — a sharp tactical mind and the makings of a detective worthy of the cowl. On paper he outclasses grown men without a trace of superhuman power. But his hardest battle is never with the enemy in front of him; it is the constant, grinding effort to hold back the assassin he was raised to be, to choose restraint when instinct screams for the finishing blow. That is the true measure of Robin — not that a child can fight like an assassin, but that this one fights every day to be something better than the weapon he was born to be.

"I am Robin. Son of Batman. And that's all you need to know!"

For all his talk of birthright, the thing Damian wants most is the approval of the one man who refuses to simply hand it to him. Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.2 July 11, 2026
  • Rework Synergies converted to the new bond system. The previous flat stat-card synergies were rebuilt as typed bond grants: Blood of the Bat (w/ Batman) now grants the Dynamic Special The Dynamic Duo and the New Passive Unrestrained Rage and Grief; Siblings of Shadow (w/ Batgirl) now grants the Upgraded Special Sweeping Through Shadow.
  • Rework Corrected the Best Partners tooltip for the Batman bond to read "Blood of the Bat" (previously mislabeled "The Dynamic Duo," which is the combo granted inside that bond, not the bond itself).

Documentation/transparency entry for the roster-wide synergy revamp. Robin's bonds were migrated from the old one-line stat cards to the new typed-grant format (New Passive / Dynamic Special / Upgraded Special) at values consistent with the new framework. The earlier v1.0.1 note describing "The Dynamic Duo" as a flat "+8% ATK/MAG" passive reflects the pre-revamp synergy and no longer matches the current bond. His base kit is unchanged.

v1.0.1 May 22, 2026
  • Nerf EVA reduced from 50% to 42%.
  • Rework Default loadout reshuffled to lean Booster: Lurking in the Shadows and A Proper Family promoted to active passives; Youthful and Swift Movements and Upbringing of an Assassin moved to reserve. New Habits promoted to active ULTIMATUM; Old Habits moved to reserve.
  • Rework Upbringing of an Assassin shortened from four bullets to two — same mechanical effect, far less rules text: "+20% ATK (+15% more when below 50% HP). No longer applies buffs to allies."
  • Buff Old Habits now doubles "Bleed" duration (2 → 4 turns) when used while in "Stealth", rewarding the kit's signature setup.
  • Rework The Dynamic Duo synergy bullet 2 reworded for clarity: "If one Champion is defeated, the surviving Champion gains +10% ATK and MAG."
  • Nerf League of Assassins costume effect "Strike to Kill" reduced from +10% to +8% CRIT RATE.

Robin shipped labeled Booster but defaulting to a near-Striker loadout, with one of his active passives (Upbringing of an Assassin) explicitly disabling the Booster portions of three other abilities. This pass leans into the dual-build identity by making the default actually Booster: he supports the Bat-Family by default, and re-spec'ing into Striker is a deliberate, meaningful choice. EVA and the League costume were trimmed to keep the dodge-crit ceiling from being overtuned. Old Habits gains a Stealth payoff so the Striker re-spec ULT actually rewards his signature playstyle. None of the kit's identity changed — just which side of it is on by default.
