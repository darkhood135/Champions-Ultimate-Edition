Summoner

Awakened with no name, no past, and no memory of the world they woke into, the Summoner fights to hold a fracturing multiverse together — and to learn what they truly are.

Every other hero is built on something remembered: a murdered father, a lost world, a lesson learned too late. The Summoner has none of it. They opened their eyes into a reality already coming apart at the seams — worlds bleeding into one another, twisted by a gathering darkness that turns villains into instruments and realities into ruins — and they faced it with nothing behind them at all. No name they could recall. No home to defend. No wound to avenge. Where others fight to honor a past, the Summoner has only the present and the choices made in it, and so they built themselves forward, one decision at a time. Standing beside a wall-crawler from a shattered New York and a shadow out of a broken Gotham — men whose every act is anchored to a grief they carry openly — they found no memory of their own to match, and took something better instead: a code to live by, chosen rather than inherited. Yet in the quiet after every battle, something stirs far beneath: an ancient ache with no source, a call in a voice that feels almost familiar, and the certainty that somewhere in the emptiness where their past should be, a piece of them is missing.

Their power revealed itself the way everything else has — all at once, in the middle of a fight they had no business surviving. Reaching for help they did not have, the Summoner tore open the space between worlds and pulled, dragging allies across the void to stand at their side; and when the enemy pressed, light answered their call unbidden, radiant and searing, driving back the darkness like a held breath released. Every use since has taught them the same unsettling lesson: the limit they expected to find isn't there. The deeper they reach, the more there is to reach for, as though the power is not something they are learning but something they are remembering. And beneath the light, when they push too far, the other thing shifts — a vast and patient darkness that answers just as readily, and does not feel like temptation at all. It feels like coming home.

"I... I remember."

No Questions Asked w/ Spider-Man

The first person the Summoner ever met never once asked what they were. Peter Parker simply made room at his side, threw a joke into the space where an introduction should have gone, and fought alongside them as though they had always been there — offering a nameless stranger the one thing no one else could: somewhere to belong before they'd done anything to earn it. Whatever the Summoner eventually learns about themselves, this will always remain the first kindness they ever knew, and a fair part of the reason they believe these worlds are worth holding together at all.

Nothing But Questions w/ Batman

Batman gave the Summoner nothing for free. Where the wall-crawler offered a hand, the Dark Knight offered scrutiny — a hundred questions they had no answers to, trust parceled out an inch at a time and never one inch further than it had been earned. The Summoner doesn't resent him for it. Of all the minds in all the worlds now bleeding together, Bruce Wayne may be the only one capable of uncovering what they truly are, and that is exactly what makes standing beside him equal parts hope and dread — because some quiet part of the Summoner isn't certain they want the answer.

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.1 July 11, 2026
  • Nerf MAX HP reduced 10,000 → 9,000.
  • Nerf Memories Shattered (Spider-Man synergy) — team buff reduced from +10% → +4% ATK, MAG, DEF and RES to all allies.
  • Nerf Bounds Broken (Batman synergy) — scaling ceiling reduced from +150% → +50% ATK and MAG.
  • Buff Unknown Depths of Power (Core Passive) — now grants 25% ULT for every 3 turns passed since the start of battle, replacing the flat nullification of passive ULT gain.
  • Buff Path of Protection — added: "Reduces damage received by an additional 5% while a summon is active (must be summoned by self)."
  • Rework Portal to an Alternate Reality — added a replacement clause: summoning a Champion Mimic while one is already on the battlefield defeats the existing Mimic and spawns the new one normally. Only one Mimic may be active at a time.
  • Rework Searing Light and Sanctifying Light — chain falloff wording clarified. The reduction is subtracted from the initial value per chain (300% / 250% / 200% / 150% / 100% / 50%), not halved, with a floor of 50%. No functional change.

Both synergies released well above the established bond power budget: Memories Shattered granted an unconditional four-stat team buff stronger than most Leader abilities, and Bounds Broken's +150% ceiling was triple the cap set on comparable stacking passives elsewhere in the roster. Both are now in line. The HP trim matters specifically because the Champion Mimic is destroyed when The Summoner falls — that kill-switch is only meaningful counterplay if The Summoner is realistically killable, and at 10,000 HP behind a flat 10% damage reduction he was not. The core's ULT change fixes a hidden build-lock: with passive generation fully nullified, A Shutter Within was the only route to an ULTIMATUM and was therefore mandatory rather than chosen; a slow independent trickle frees the slot while keeping his ultimate a deliberate climb. Path of Protection gains a rider so all four Paths offer a mechanical hook rather than a flat stat line, and it ties his most defensive Path to his summoning identity. The Portal clause closes a stacking loophole, and the chain wording now matches the established falloff phrasing used elsewhere.
