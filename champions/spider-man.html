Spider-Man

Peter Parker

Striker

Bitten by a radioactive spider and bound by the lesson that great power demands great responsibility, Peter Parker guards the streets of New York as the friendly neighborhood hero known as the Amazing Spider-Man.

For all the wonders that crawl and soar through New York, the city's truest guardian is a kid from Queens who never asked to be extraordinary. Peter Parker was a bright, overlooked science student until a radioactive spider changed the shape of his life — and until a single act of selfishness cost him his Uncle Ben and taught him the lesson he has carried ever since: that great power demands great responsibility. Where others fight for glory or country, Spider-Man fights out of conscience, driven by the quiet certainty that anyone who can help and chooses not to is answerable for what follows. It is why he shows up when no one is watching and no reward is waiting, why he throws himself between the innocent and things far stronger than himself, and why — bruised, exhausted, and outmatched — he refuses to stay down. Beneath the wisecracks lies a young man who has lost more than most and keeps giving anyway, and the jokes he never stops telling are less bravado than armor, the only thing he can afford to bring into a fight that won't cost someone else.

That defiance is matched by a body remade for it. The spider's bite granted Parker proportionate strength enough to lift many times his own weight, reflexes and agility that let him flow through a battle like water, and the near-uncanny "spider-sense" that warns him of danger before it strikes. He clings to sheer walls and ceilings as easily as walking, and swings across the skyline on webbing of his own design — the work of a scientific mind as formidable as his physique, forever engineering the edge that keeps him a step ahead of foes who outclass him on paper. But it is not the powers that make him the Amazing Spider-Man. It is that he uses them, again and again, for the friendly neighborhood that never knows his name.

"With great power, comes great responsibility."

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.0.2 July 11, 2026
  • Nerf ATK reduced 390 → 340.
  • Nerf Evasion pass — base EVA reduced 35% → 15%; Spider-Sense full-evade chance reduced 25% → 20%; Sense Tingle EVA buff reduced +25% → +15%.
  • Nerf Crawler Assault — cooldown increased 1 → 2 turns.
  • Rework Synergies converted to the new bond system — each partner bond now grants typed abilities (New Passive, Dynamic Special, Upgraded Special, New Special, Upgraded ULTIMATUM) rather than a flat stat bonus.

As the first Champion implemented, Spider-Man sat above the power curve the rest of the roster later settled on. An all-rounder identity is intended — solid in most departments rather than a fragile glass cannon — but he was peaking in too many at once: elite durability stacked with elite evasion and high sustained damage, with no real weakness to play around. This pass trims the evasion stack (so he is evasive, not untouchable) and raises Crawler Assault's cooldown so his single-target damage is a strong rotation rather than an every-turn 600% ATK output, while leaving his durability, mobility, and burst ceiling intact. Synergies were also migrated to the new bond framework introduced roster-wide.

v1.0.1 May 19, 2026
  • Nerf Spider Strength — bonus damage per 10 EN remaining reduced from 100% ATK → 50% ATK.
  • Rework Web Swing — can now target a panel in addition to an obstacle. Panel-targeting relocates Spider-Man 5 panels in the direction of the targeted panel.

At full EN, Spider Strength was capable of dealing close to 1,800% ATK on a single target — disproportionately powerful versus comparable ULTIMATUMs. The new scaling caps the burst around 1,300% ATK at full EN, keeping the empty-the-tank fantasy without one-shotting tanks. Web Swing was a clean mobility tool but completely dead on obstacle-free stages; the panel-direction option ensures it has utility on any battlefield.
