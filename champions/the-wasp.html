The Wasp

Janet Van Dyne

Healer

Janet Van Dyne named the Avengers. Most people don't remember that. She was a founding member, a team leader, and the one who held the line when everyone else had drifted — and none of that history slows her down when she fights. The Pym Particle technology is hers as much as it was ever Hank's: she shrinks into strike range faster than anything her size has a right to move, or expands into something the battlefield has to reorganize itself around. Her wasp sting is a precision bioelectric discharge, scaled from a tap that paralyzes to a pulse that empties EN reserves and puts everything in a five-panel radius on the ground. The real thing to understand about Janet is that she heals without stopping. Overhealth on one ally, a full restoration on another, her core passive banking credit every time she does it and feeding it back into Sting's damage ceiling. She keeps her team alive and dangerous at the same time. She was doing this before most of them were heroes. She knows how long a fight actually takes.

"A hundred years from now, when we're all dead and buried, there will be only two things that we're remembered for... what we did. And what we looked like. Our uniforms are as much a part of our legacy as anything we'll ever do."

Changelog

Balance changes since this Champion's release. Newest first.

v1.1.0 May 27, 2026
  • Nerf Expert of Pym Particles and Bioenergy (Core Passive) — Sting's per-heal bonus reduced from 100% → 70% MAG; maximum bonus reduced from +500% → +350% MAG. Maximum Sting damage is now 650% MAG (down from 800%).
  • Nerf Expert of Pym Particles and Bioenergy (Core Passive) — EN refund per heal or Overhealth grant reduced from 10 → 8 EN.
  • Rework Stinging Surge — Target now also loses 1 STA in addition to the existing 5 EN penalty, spreading the cost across two resources to better reflect the "sting" of the application.

Sting's damage ceiling at 800% MAG placed a Healer above most Strikers' peak single-target output when fully charged, even accounting for the opportunity cost of banking heals. The reduced cap keeps the scaling mechanic intact and rewarding while landing at a more appropriate ceiling for the role. The EN refund reduction is a measured trim for consistency with the adjusted Sting economy. The Stinging Surge STA penalty better represents the bioelectric cost of the application without making it prohibitively expensive for the recipient.
